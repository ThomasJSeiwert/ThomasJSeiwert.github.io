Toilet Water Conservation Flush Kit


The International Houseware Association asked Industrial Design students at University of Illinois at Chicago to compete in their Student Design Competition. Each student needed to develop an innovative household item.

Toilet Water Conservation Flush Kit


The International Houseware Association asked Industrial Design students at University of Illinois at Chicago to compete in their Student Design Competition. Each student needed to develop an innovative household item.




As you can see, the statistics for wasted water from toilet flushing is shockingly high. Dual Flush Toilets exist to remedy this issue, however they can be expensive and a haste to install.



As you can see above, the average toilet works by pressing the handle, which pulls the arm and lifts the flapper. The water gets sucked from the bowl and the water from the tank begins to refill the bowl. The float ball tells the tank how much water to use for the bowl. The tank then refills itself and is ready for the next use.






My concepts involved manually lifting the flapper from the tank via button or switch. My flappers were designed with hinges to hold the stick that would connect to the button or switch. This would allow the user direct control over how much water to use for each flush.
